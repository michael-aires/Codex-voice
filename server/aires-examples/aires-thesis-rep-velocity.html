The rep velocity thesis

Product thesis · CRM for pre-sale sales teams · June 2026 · Confidential

A salesperson wants the highest impact in the least time. Every feature we ship either earns a minute back or wastes one. The thesis: a CRM that turns data in into leverage out — fast enough to keep, smart enough to act above human scale.

The core bet

The job is to get it done, fast

Reps don't want a system of record. They want a system of action. The unit of value isn't a clean database — it's a sold-firm unit, and before that, the next right move made faster than a competitor's. So the product is judged on a brutal exchange rate: minutes spent in the tool versus impact taken out of it.

Two questions sit underneath everything a rep does in a day. If the CRM answers them instantly, it wins. If it makes the rep go find the answer, it loses.

"What should I do today?"   "How do I know I'm done?"

Most CRMs answer neither. They show everything and prioritize nothing — leaving the rep to be the query engine. Our job is to be the query engine, so the rep is only ever the closer.

The flywheel

Data in, leverage out

The reason reps avoid data entry is that traditional CRMs make it a tax with no rebate — you feed the machine and the machine feeds a report your manager reads. We invert it. Every input the rep makes immediately sharpens what they get back: better prioritization, tighter lists, faster surfacing of the deal that's about to slip.

Rep logs activity → CRM enriches the picture → Better "do this next" → Rep acts & wins faster ↻

The flywheel only spins if the rebate is felt the same day. That is the design constraint: data entered this morning has to change what the tool tells the rep to do this afternoon. The more they put in, the more they get out — and the loop becomes self-reinforcing rather than self-defeating.

Why velocity, not just results

Results lag — so we measure the leading edge

In pre-sale real estate, a result — a unit sold firm — can be weeks or months downstream of the work that earned it. If reps and managers only watch results, they are steering by a rear-view mirror. So the product makes the leading indicators — daily and weekly velocity of the right activities — first-class and visible. Two stakeholders, two lenses on the same data.

Sales manager

Drive output

Needs to see velocity across the team, spot who has stalled, and know the pipeline is being worked — before results confirm it. Manages by the leading edge.

Sales rep

Prove performance now

Can't wait for closings to show they are effective. Needs their daily and weekly velocity to be legible and self-evident — performance you can see today, not next quarter.

The corollary: make all activity reportable

If velocity is the currency, then every meaningful action a rep takes must be captured as a countable, attributable event. Not for surveillance — for credit. Activity that isn't reportable is work the rep did for free and can't be rewarded or coached on.

Operating above human scale

The needle in the haystack

A single project can carry thousands of leads — more than any human can hold in their head or work by hand. At that scale the bottleneck isn't effort, it's attention allocation. The winning rep isn't the one who works hardest; it's the one who consistently finds the few leads that matter today and ignores the thousands that don't.

So the product's central capability is narrowing — turning an unworkable haystack into a short, ranked, trustworthy list. It's built as a ladder, each rung removing more manual effort. The same ladder runs in two directions: the system can climb it for the rep, or the rep can climb it themselves when they arrive with an intent.

1

Search — find

Pull any contact or signal out of the pile on demand. The floor capability.

2

Filter — slice

Reduce the haystack to a defined segment by attributes, stage, recency, or source.

3

Saved filters — remember

Stop rebuilding the same slice every morning. The rep's working views become durable.

4

Smart Lists ✦ — decide for me

The system maintains living, prioritized lists that surface the needle automatically — the answer to "what should I do today" rendered as a list the rep just works top-down.

Two ways to find the needle

The system can narrow for you — or you narrow it yourself

The ladder runs both directions, and the product needs both. Sometimes the system should hand the rep the list (push); sometimes the rep arrives already knowing who they want and needs to carve that list out fast (pull).

Push

"What should I do today?"

The system surfaces the needle — a ranked Smart List waiting on the dashboard. The rep just works it.

Pull

"I know who I want — help me reach them."

An intent arrives: a promo, a fresh event, a colleague away. The rep composes a filter, builds the list, and acts.

The pull job is timely outreach

The pull loop is intent → filter → list → outreach: turn a reason-to-reach into a precise segment, then communicate with the whole segment without leaving the CRM — every touch logged. A handful of asks recur: "everyone who showed interest in a one-bedroom in the last month," "anyone we haven't contacted since yesterday's event," "Jason's A and B prospects while he's away," "everyone who opened the two-bedroom promo." They sound different; they're the same move.

Eight composable dimensions

Those asks reduce to a small, composable set — attribute, recency window, contact gap, event / behavior, source, owner, grade, engagement — combined with AND/OR and saved as a Smart List the rep can re-run. Build the set once and most future asks fall out of it for free.

One consequence worth stating up front: the moment a list can bulk-message, it becomes a compliance surface. Consent and opt-out (CASL) and SMS A2P limits have to be a filter dimension and a hard send-time gate — not an afterthought.

Where it lives

Three surfaces, three jobs

The thesis shows up on exactly three surfaces. Each does a distinct job in the daily loop, and each must answer one of the two questions or capture reportable activity.

Dashboard
The command center. Velocity, what's hot, what's slipping, what to do next."What should I do today?"
Contact table
The narrowing & list-building surface. Search, filter on eight dimensions, save, Smart Lists, and launch outreach against the result."Show me — and let me reach — the needles."
Contact profile
The action surface. Respond fast, log activity, advance the deal — every action reportable."How do I know I'm done?"

What good looks like

Done is a number, not a feeling

The product succeeds when a rep can open it, be told what to do, do it, and watch a reportable velocity number move — all before they have spent ten minutes administering anything. Manager and rep look at the same leading-edge truth. The haystack is permanently workable. And the flywheel pays its rebate the same day.

The CRM stops being where work goes to be recorded, and becomes where work goes to get done.
