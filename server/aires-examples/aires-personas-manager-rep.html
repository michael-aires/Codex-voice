Personas · June 2026 · Manager vs. rep

Both watch the same leading-edge data — because results lag, velocity is the shared currency. They just need different views of it.

SM

The sales manager

Goal · drive output
Goals
  • Keep the whole team's output high and consistent.
  • See velocity across reps before results confirm it.
  • Spot a stalled rep or cooling pipeline early.
Frustrations
  • Can only judge the team by closings that arrive too late to coach on.
  • No honest, real-time read on whether the pipeline is being worked.
Behaviors
  • Manages by the leading edge — daily and weekly activity, not just the board.
  • Wants every meaningful action reportable and attributable.
  • Expects campaign & event follow-up to actually happen, and an away rep's book to stay worked.
Watches
Team velocity & coverage — who is working what, how fast
"I need to know we're winning before the closings tell me."
SR

The sales rep

Goal · prove performance now
Goals
  • Highest impact in the least time — get it done.
  • Find and respond to the hot lead before anyone else.
  • Show they are effective today, not next quarter.
Frustrations
  • Thousands of leads, no clear "what should I do today."
  • When I have a reason to reach a segment, I'm stuck exporting to a spreadsheet and messaging elsewhere.
  • Effort feels invisible because results lag — hard to prove worth.
  • Data entry that costs time and gives nothing back.
Behaviors
  • Works a trusted, ranked list top-down rather than the whole pile.
  • When a promo or event hits, builds a filtered list and runs outreach against the whole segment.
  • Logs activity if — and only if — it sharpens their next move.
Watches
My daily & weekly velocity — performance I can see today
"Tell me what to do, let me close it, and show my number move."
