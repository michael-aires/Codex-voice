Jobs to be done — the sales rep

JTBD canvas · June 2026 · Rep velocity thesis

Job
statement
When I start my day facing thousands of leads, I want to know exactly what to work and when I'm done — so I can get the highest impact in the least time.
Context — when does this job arise?situation
Pre-sale real estate; a single project can carry thousands of leads — above human scale.
Results lag by weeks or months, so today's effort can't be judged by today's closings.
A manager is watching output; the rep has to prove they are effective now.
Current solutions, and why they fall shortworkarounds
Generic CRM that shows everything and prioritizes nothing — rep becomes the query engine.
Memory, gut feel, and hand-built spreadsheets remade every morning.
Data entry feels like a tax with no rebate — feeds a report, not the rep.
When I have a reason to reach a segment, I export to a spreadsheet and message from another tool — slow, and nothing gets logged back.
Functional jobsdoes
Find the needle in the haystack — the few leads that matter today.
Respond to hot leads as fast as possible, before they cool.
Turn an intent into a precise list — compose interest, recency, owner, grade, engagement — when I already know who I want.
Communicate with a whole segment in place (bulk or sequenced) and have it logged.
Follow up on the right contacts at the right time.
Log activity without it slowing me down.
Know when the day's work is actually complete.
Emotional jobsfeels
Feel in control, not buried by volume.
Feel the closure of "done" — not an endless, formless list.
Feel my effort counts and is credited, not invisible.
Trust the list — believe the system surfaced the right things.
Social jobsseen as
Be seen by my manager as productive — today, not next quarter.
Prove my velocity with legible, self-evident numbers.
Be recognized as a top performer on the team.
Desired outcomes — what "hiring the product" looks likesuccess
A short, ranked, trustworthy list waiting every morning — "what should I do today" answered for me.
A same-day rebate: data I enter this morning changes what the tool tells me this afternoon.
Every meaningful action captured as reportable, attributable activity — credit, not surveillance.
Velocity visible to me and my manager — "done" is a number that moves, not a feeling.
When I arrive with an intent, intent → list in seconds → outreach in place → auto-logged, even on a teammate's book.
